About

PhD in Astrophysics with extensive experience in independently leading research projects and the development of sophisticated data analysis software. My strong academic background in Astronomy, Physics, and Data Analysis helps me to quickly understand complicated concepts, learn state-of-the-art methodologies, and grasp the results of current research. I acquired extensive technical writing and presentation skills, in particular through authoring scientific publications and talking at international conferences, (invited) seminars, and colloquia.

Research

My research interests are near-field cosmology, resolved stellar populations and Galactic archaeology. My current work focuses on using NIR photometric data from the VISTA near-infrared survey of the Magellanic Cloud system (VMC) and the VISTA Hemisphere Survey (VHS) to investigate the morphology of the Magellanic Clouds and find substructures that might be related to their interaction history, while also characterizing them kinematically and eventually chemically using spectroscopic data from FLAMES (VLT) in combination with astrometry from Gaia Early DR3.

Before joining the the Leibniz Institute of Astrophysics (AIP) as a Research Scientist, I obtained my M.sc degree in Astrophysics and High Energy Physics from the Cadi Ayyad University in Marrakech, my M.sc thesis focused on the atmospheric dynamics of RR Lyrae from a spectroscopic point of view by obtaining a detailed analysis of several shock wave signature variations over different pulsation cycles and the implication of the Blazhko effect on their evolution.
